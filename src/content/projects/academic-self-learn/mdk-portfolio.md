## Overview

A personal portfolio site built during college as a self-directed project. Designed and implemented from scratch to practice front-end fundamentals and express my own approach to layout, structure, and presentation. The site included clear sections for who I am, what I’ve done, my skills, my work, and how to get in touch.

## What I did

- Designed and built the layout and all pages from my own ideas.
- Used HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for structure, styling, and interactivity.
- Hosted as a GitHub Pages demo for sharing and reference.

## Site structure and content

### About me / What I have done

A section introducing myself and summarizing what I had done so far—background, interests, and goals—so visitors could quickly understand who I am and what I was working toward.

### Skills during college

A dedicated area listing the technologies and skills I was learning and using in college (e.g. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and any tools or practices I was practicing at the time).

### Works during college time

A section showcasing projects, assignments, or other work I completed during college—each with a short description and, where relevant, links or screenshots.

### Separate projects page

A dedicated **Projects** page that listed my projects in one place, with links, so all my college and self-learn work was easy to find.

### Contact page

A **Contact** page with a way for visitors to reach me—e.g. email, social links, or a simple contact form—so anyone could get in touch.

## Takeaways

- Learned to plan and implement a multi-page portfolio without a framework.
- Gained practice in responsive layout and clean, maintainable markup.
- Structured content around clear sections: about, skills, work, projects, and contact.
